the money problem

Our ancestors invented money to facilitate exchange. It has been very useful for building our civilisation. However, today we have largely forgotten its intended purpose. Both for who he was and what he said, Cortés strikes at the heart of the problem: craving as a fundamental human trait, and our promotion of wealth to an end in itself.

an end in itself

Money is only a proxy for the fruits of our efforts. We see the superstar, and conflate their money with contentment. Even if they truly are content, their contentment is their comfort and freedom, not their money. Almost all of humanity must dedicate their life’s efforts to gaining basic comforts of shelter and food. What potential, what creativity, what happiness, might be revealed if we all had space to breathe, to think, about how to channel our life’s attention? We support projects that work on realising this dream.

craving

The human reaction to pleasure is craving for more. This makes sense in evolutionary terms. Our ancestors were continuously compelled to search for food. Today, we can convert money into pleasure. our civilisation is as enslaved by money as the alcoholic by alcohol. Some of us have more than we could ever need. Most of us have none, and desparately need it to survive.
